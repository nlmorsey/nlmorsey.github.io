Reflections on Software Testing

Qualities of an excellent software tester:

  • analytical
  • assertive
  • diplomatic
  • honest
  • enthusiastic
  • curious

Here are some of the things that I have learned, thanks to people who have been kind enough to talk to me about software testing.

An excellent software tester seeks above all things to "break" the software: she looks for edge cases.
She solves problems. She thinks from beginning to end: gathering, planning, testing, and assessing.
She asks many questions. She ensures that she knows enough about the details of the software in order to test it effectively.
She is not afraid to ask for information.
She speaks up.
She works as part of a team. She understands where the developers are coming from.
She does not promise what she cannot deliver, or misrepresent what she has tested.
She is always learning. She keeps up to date on software practices, and learns about the software she is testing, and learns from experience.
She doesn't exaggerate what she can do.
She knows about code, but doesn't try to do the job of the developers or the designers.
She puts the customer and the product quality first, and she does not cut corners.
She is engaged, and has a can-do attitude.
She advocates the resolution of bugs.
