Reflections on Software Testing

Here are some of the things that I have learned, thanks to people who have been kind enough to talk to me about software testing.

Qualities of an excellent software tester:

  • collaborative: works as part of a team, not alone; prioritizes group not self
  • curious: explores, asks questions, pays attention, learns constantly
  • attentive: notices small details that could lead to important problems
  • thorough: gathers enough details about the software in order to test it effectively
  • committed: seeks above all else to "break" the software, looks for edge cases
  • analytical: solves problems, thinks through a test case from beginning to end
  • clear: communicates precisely, and clearly explains how to replicate bugs so as to save developers time
  • persuasive: advocates the resolution of important bugs
  • concise
  • assertive: speaks up
  • diplomatic: talks about bugs and brings up issues sensitively
  • respectful: understands where the developers are coming from
  • honest: promises only what can be delivered and represents accurately what has been tested
  • enthusiastic: stays engaged
  • organized: prioritizes the most important tests and the most important bugs
  • ethical: can make the right judgment calls
  • value-driven: prioritizes the customer
  • empathetic: respects developers and designers
  • educated: understands the product, and keeps up-to-date on software testing
  • communicative: asks questions, asks for more information, and brings up issues early enough during the project
  • reflective: learns from experience
  • efficient: able to test the most important things first and frontline the most urgent bugs
